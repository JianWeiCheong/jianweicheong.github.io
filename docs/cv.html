    • Variations in statistical complexity of genome sequences across species
      • Computational and Systems Biology, NTU course
      • Analyzed genome sequences of human, chimpanzee, rhesus macaque, dog, and fruit fly, from GenBank assembly in R.
      • Applied the Baum-Welch algorithm and Akaike information criterion to compute the average statistical complexity of their genomes.
      • Showed that \(S(\text{fruit fly}) > S(\text{dog}) > S(\text{primate})\) with low p-value, where \(S\) is the statistical complexity.
      • Suggested that increasing biological complexity correspond to less statistical complexity in genomes, i.e., less entropy and more stability in genome’s randomness.
