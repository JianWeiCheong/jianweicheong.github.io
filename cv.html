Research and Projects

Efficiency of Maxwell Demon as Ratchet System subjected to both a Heat and Information Reservoir

  • Description of project
    In this research, we investigate into a ratchet system that exploits both a heat and information reservoir to extract work and acts as an engine. Such a system, without the information reservoir, is an equilibrium system and will not do work as otherwise it will become a perpetual motion machine of the second kind. However, the inclusion of the information reservoir could drive the system away from equilibrium, and causes it to do useful work. This project explores the use of Markov model of different complexity as information reservoir, and evaluates the effect of the complexity of the model on the efficiency of the resulting engine. In addition, it is known that a ratchet system can be driven into non-equilibrium states by considering it as a flashing ratchet system or when it is subjected to chaotic noise solely with the presence of the heat bath. Here, we shall investigate whether the efficiency of such a non-equilibrium ratchet system can be further enhanced by employing information reservoir that implements Markov model of different complexity.
  • Bachelor's Final Year Project
  • Thesis: The Cost of Information Processing in an Information Ratchet System
  • Short paper

Characterization of quantum wavefunctions in a tight-binding lattice

  • Description of project
    Numerical study of the conductance fluctuations in the SSH and Haldane models. Python is used along with “Kwant” package to simulate electron transports across the 1D SSH chain and the 2D honeycomb lattice of the Haldane model. Disorders are sampled randomly and fluctuations in conductance are calculated. Topological edge states are visualised.
  • Undergraduate Research Experience in CAmpus (URECA), NTU
  • Paper: Conductance Fluctuations in the SSH and Haldane Models
  • Poster: Numerical Study of Conductance Properties of 2D Topological Insulators

Monte Carlo Photon Transport in Multi-layered Biological Tissues

  • Description of project
    Python is used to simulate photon transports in biological tissues by means of random walk. Different biological tissues are defined by their experimentally obtained scattering coefficients, absorption coefficients, refractive index and anisotropy. Variables such as reflectance and absorbance are calculated and visualised. The project is a demonstration of the applications of computational methods on medical areas such as biomedical imaging and photon therapy.
  • Computational Physics Course, NTU
  • This project was done together with Elaine Chiu, who specialises in Medical Physics and provided the physical knowledge of photon transports in biologically tissues.
  • Report: Monte Carlo Photon Transport in Multi-layered Biological Tissues

Numerical study of the accuracy of Fermi’s golden rule in a spherically symmetric well

  • Description of project

Hovering Drone

  • Description of project
    A simple quadcopter drone is designed and programmed from scratch. An Arduino microcontroller is programmed for pulse width modulation (PWM) output based on a ultrasonic sensor input. The printed circuit board (PCB) is designed and printed via Autodesk EAGLE and the drone is designed and 3D printed via Autodesk Fusion360. The 3D model is also imported to COMSOL Multiphysics to simulate the physical system.
